### 🎯 Strategic Overview
As an **Industrial Civil Engineer**, I applied the **DMAIC** methodology to solve high downtime rates in a textile production environment. This project focused on identifying systemic mechanical failures and warp breakage patterns to optimize the primary production line's capacity.

### 1. The Challenge (Friction Analysis)
* **Problem:** Weaving machines (SOMET/VAMATEX) exhibited downtime rates exceeding 33%, causing production bottlenecks.
* **Impact:** Reduced operational efficiency and increased quality costs relative to sales revenue.

### 2. The Purpose (Strategic Objective)
* **Diagnosis:** Utilize multi-level Pareto analysis to isolate looms with the highest failure frequency.
* **Optimization:** Calculate ideal throughput (passes/second) to establish a baseline for machinery renewal (CAPEX).
* **Continuous Improvement:** Implement a DMAIC framework to stabilize the production cycle.

### 3. Key Results (Impact Metrics)
* **Root Cause Found:** Traced 95.8% of SOMET section failures to two specific units (Looms 1 & 4).
* **Capacity Baseline:** Established an ideal section capacity of 45.1 passes/second.
* **Financial Justification:** Provided the cost-benefit analysis for a 2.1% productivity increase through targeted equipment upgrades.

---

### 🛠️ Technical Architecture
* **Analysis Engine:** Python-based Pareto generator for failure frequency identification.
* **Methodology:** DMAIC (Define, Measure, Analyze, Improve, Control) for root cause resolution.
* **Cost Analysis:** Statistical comparison of quality costs vs. revenue.
