### 🎯 Strategic Overview
Developed a mathematical framework to solve the "Timetabling Problem" in educational institutions. The project bridges the gap between financial sustainability and customer (student) satisfaction by utilizing Pareto frontier analysis.

### 1. The Challenge (Complexity Analysis)
* **Problem:** NP-Hard scheduling problem involving multiple conflicting objectives.
* **Impact:** High administrative overhead and potential financial loss due to sub-optimal resource allocation.

### 2. The Purpose (Strategic Objective)
* **Optimization:** Utilize Integer Linear Programming to automate student-to-course assignments.
* **Decision Support:** Provide a Pareto-based interface to evaluate trade-offs between profit and satisfaction.
* **Data Governance:** Implement an Excel-VBA system for robust enrollment management.

### 3. Key Results (Impact Metrics)
* **Profit Optimization:** Identified scenarios increasing utility from $80k to $260k via strategic assignment.
* **Model Accuracy:** 100% adherence to institutional constraints (capacity, teacher availability, and student preferences).
* **Efficiency:** Transitioned from manual coordination to automated, non-deterministic polynomial time solving.
